What is Data Logger?

Data loggers monitor various systems and processes by recording data points over a timed interval to document conditions. This data acquisition ensures that processes are working properly and comply with regulatory requirements. Data loggers are especially useful for applications in remote or unattended locations, measuring temperature, flow, pressure, voltage, current, and more.


Applications

Environmental Monitoring

Data loggers are used to monitor environmental conditions such as temperature, humidity, and atmospheric pressure in industries like agriculture, food and beverage, pharmaceuticals, and HVAC systems.

Energy Management

Data loggers are used to monitor energy usage in buildings, factories, and other facilities. They can help identify areas of inefficiency and help reduce energy consumption.

Process Control

Data loggers are used to monitor and control industrial processes such as manufacturing and production lines, chemical processing, and water treatment plants. They help ensure that processes are running smoothly and efficiently.

Research and Development

Data loggers are used in scientific research to collect data on various parameters such as temperature, pressure, and light intensity. They help researchers understand complex systems and make data-driven decisions.

Transportation

Data loggers are used to monitor the conditions of goods during transportation, such as temperature and humidity, to ensure that they are transported in the optimal conditions.

Health and Safety

Data loggers are used to monitor environmental conditions in workplaces to ensure that workers are not exposed to dangerous levels of pollutants, chemicals, or radiation.
